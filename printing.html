    How does designing work?


    Design thinking typically involves five key stages:
  • Empathise: Understand the users' needs, wants, and limitations by gathering information through research and observation.
  • Define: Spot, frame, and fully understand the problem at hand.
  • Ideate: Create a list of possible solutions.
  • Prototype: Create prototypes in CAD programs of the most promising ideas.
  • Test: Gather feedback from users and use it to improve the design further.

Design stage 1 : Empathise

Write or draw what you want to 3D print today on a piece of paper. When you are finished please inform your teacher about your idea.

Design stage 2 : Define

Elaborate your design like how it will look like, feel like, it's different side.

Design stage 3 : Ideate

Use a playdoh, paper cutting, origami or any other method that make you feel like working on your model successful when you are 3D designing in Tinkercad.

Design stage 4 : Prototype

This stage you will design it in Tinkercad and keep iterating your design until you find the acceptable solution. Also 3D print your design at this stage.

Design stage 5 : Test

Show the working or usefulness of the design that you have 3D printed and designed.

Remember

In case of failure of your design always remember you will have to go back to your stage 1.
